Arrive Incheon — Terminal 1
Landing routine at ICN Terminal 1:
  1. Fill the e-Arrival Card online (e-arrivalcard.go.kr) within 3 days before landing — skips the paper form.
  2. Follow signs to Immigration → fingerprints + photo, a couple of quick questions.
  3. Collect your bags at baggage claim.
  4. Activate your eSIM (or grab a tourist SIM / WOWPASS card at the arrivals hall).
  5. Buy T-money cards and top up ~₩30,000 each at a CU / GS25 convenience store.
ArrivalT-moneyeSIM
~10:45
Head to WECO STAY Gangnam
ICN → WECO STAY (Banpo) — two easy ways:
  1. AREX + subway (~70 min): AREX train (All-Stop, T-money OK, or Express) to Seoul Station → transfer to Line 9 → ride to Sinbanpo or Express Bus Terminal → 5–10 min walk to the hotel.
  2. Or airport limousine bus (~80 min, easiest with luggage): from the ICN arrivals hall, take a bus toward the Express Bus Terminal / Banpo area → short walk or taxi to the hotel.
  3. At the hotel: official check-in is 16:00 — drop bags in luggage storage (1F, Room 103, from 10:00) and head straight back out.

🛂 Entry to Korea

For Singapore passport holders

  • Visa-free entry — Singapore passports get 90 days in Korea, no visa needed
  • K-ETA: SG passport holders have been temporarily exempt — double-check the current status at k-eta.go.kr about a month before flying, and apply (~₩10,000) if the exemption has lapsed
  • e-Arrival Card: fill it in online (e-arrivalcard.go.kr) within 3 days before landing — skips the paper form at immigration
  • Passport must be valid for the whole stay — 6+ months is the safe rule
  • Customs: declare food/agricultural items honestly; keep it simple and skip fresh produce
  • Immigration at ICN T1 is quick for visa-free entry — fingerprints + photo, routine questions

💡 Seoul Survival Guide

Everything useful before you go

🧭 From Your Hotel — Getting Around

Your base is WECO STAY (Banpo). Nearest stations: Sinbanpo (Line 9) and the big Express Bus Terminal interchange (Lines 3 · 7 · 9) — that hub reaches almost everywhere. Golden rule: open Naver Map, type the place, tap the route — it's always right. The quick direct ones:
  1. Seochon / Gyeongbokgung (Sat) — Line 3 → Gyeongbokgung, direct, ~20 min.
  2. Insadong / Anguk (Tue) — Line 3 → Anguk, direct, ~22 min.
  3. Sinsa — Acid House / Garosu-gil (Sat) — Line 3 → Sinsa, 1 stop (~4 min).
  4. Belle Studio — Maebong (Fri) — Line 3 south → Maebong, direct ~10 min, Exit 4.
  5. CheongA Clinic — Cheongdam (Thu) — Line 7 → Cheongdam, direct, ~15 min.
  6. Seongsu (Mon) — Line 7 → transfer Line 2 at Konkuk Univ → Seongsu, ~30 min.
  7. Seongbuk-dong (Sun) — Line 3 → transfer Line 4 at Chungmuro → Hansung Univ, ~35 min.
  8. Yeonnam / Hongdae — check Naver Map (Line 9 + a transfer), or a Kakao T (~30–40 min).
  9. Itaewon / Hannam — just across the river; a Kakao T taxi is quickest (~15 min).
  10. Banpo Han River — ~15-min walk south, or Sinbanpo (Line 9) Exit.

🚇 Getting Around

• T-money card — buy at any ICN convenience store, top up with cash, taps subway + bus + taxis
• Subway is king: clean, cheap (~₩1,500/ride), English signage everywhere
• Naver Map is your Google Maps — Google barely works for directions in Korea
• AREX Express: ICN ⇄ Seoul Station in 43 min · All-Stop train ~1h on T-money
• Trains stop ~midnight — check the last-train time on Naver before a late night

🚕 Taxis & Late Night

• Kakao T app — Korea's Uber; works with foreign cards, no haggling
• Base fare ~₩4,800; cross-town rides usually ₩10–20k
• Orange/silver taxis are standard; black "deluxe" cabs cost more
• After the subway closes, Kakao T surge is real but rides still come
• Airport limousine buses are the comfy luggage-friendly ICN option

💳 Money & Tax Refund

• Cards work almost everywhere — Visa/Mastercard fine; Apple Pay is hit-or-miss
• Keep some cash for markets (Gwangjang, street food)
• WOWPASS (prepaid card + T-money in one) is a handy tourist option
• Tax refund: spend ₩15,000+ at Tax Free shops — instant refund in many stores (passport needed) or kiosks at ICN
• Rough maths: ₩10,000 ≈ SGD 9.3

📱 Apps to Download

• Naver Map — navigation (set to English)
• Papago — best Korean translator, camera mode for menus
• Kakao T — taxis
• Catchtable — queue/reserve the hyped restaurants & cafes
• Klook — attraction tickets, spa & day-pass deals

🌅 Han River — Sunset + Ramyeon Night

  • Where: Banpo Hangang Park — the riverside park closest to your hotel (~15-min walk south, or a 5-min taxi). By metro it's Express Bus Terminal (L3·7·9) Exit 8-1, then through the pedestrian underpass to the river
  • When: arrive by ~17:15 — mid-October sunset is around 18:00. Grab a west-facing patch of grass while it's still light
  • Mats: rent a picnic mat (~₩5,000) from the park vendors or convenience store, or bring your own
  • The ramyeon ritual: ① buy instant ramyeon + toppings (egg, cheese, gimbap) at the park's CU/GS25 → ② pay at the coin ramyeon machine (~₩1,500, takes card/cash/T-money) → ③ empty the noodles into the metal pot and fill water to the line → ④ press start, wait ~4 min, it boils automatically. Cook it yourself, eat it by the water
  • Chimaek: you can get fried chicken delivered right to the park — note the delivery-zone number on the nearest sign and order via the shop or a delivery app (Baemin/Coupang Eats). Beer from the convenience store
  • Rainbow Fountain: Banpo Bridge's Moonlight Rainbow Fountain usually runs Apr–Oct with evening shows ~19:30 & 20:00 — check the day's schedule, as it goes weekend-only / stops for winter in late October
  • Bring: a warm layer (the riverside gets chilly after dark in October), wet wipes, and a small trash bag — bins fill up fast
  • Flexible: it's right by your hotel, so any free evening works — Tue 13 is the plan (weekday night = far fewer people than the weekend)

🍽 Eating Out — How It Works

  • No tipping — ever. Price on the menu is the price
  • Press the call button on the table to summon staff — it's not rude, it's the system
  • Banchan (side dishes) are free and refillable — just ask
  • Pay at the counter on the way out, not at the table
  • Popular spots (London Bagel Museum, Pig Restaurant 2) queue via app or waitlist — check Catchtable first
  • Some traditional restaurants are floor-seating — wear decent socks, shoes come off
  • Water and cutlery are often self-serve — look for the station or the drawer under the table

🧠 Street Smarts

  • Seoul is extremely safe, even late at night — normal city sense applies
  • Trash cans are rare — carry a small bag for wrappers
  • Keep right on escalators; let passengers off the train first
  • Subway priority seats (dark colored) stay empty even when the car is packed — don't sit there
  • Convenience stores (GS25, CU, 7-Eleven) are 24/7 lifesavers — snacks, ATMs, T-money top-up
  • October sunset is ~18:00 — plan golden-hour spots (Namsan!) accordingly
  • Still to book ~2 weeks out: Belle Studio time, dinner with Shuen, and hyped spots (London Bagel, Ikseon Atteut). CheongA Clinic is booked ✓ — Thu 15, 14:30
